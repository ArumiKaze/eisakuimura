Overview Talchul is a top-down stealth game. You play as a refugee trying to evade a labor camp. Along your journey you meet other refugees who increase your ability to see, but also increase the likelihood you'll be detected. You must choose to group with them or not as you wander through fields evading soldiers. How far can you go?
Inspiration for the game came from current world events happening in 2016. I pitched the idea of creating a game revealing the brutality of North Korea to its prisoners. My team of four people including myself programmed a game where you play as a prisoner and try to survive as long as possible during an escape attempt. The name of the game came from the Korean word "to escape".
Details
Talchul is meant to be an endless game. No matter how hard or long the player tries, the player will not be able to escape and I believed that it accurately reflected the situation in North Korea. You can stay under the radar easier if you lay low but once you start acting out, you become a bigger target for the regime to put down. After your 4th death, you get an option to "give up" and this leads to a game over screen with all your stats throughout all your retries.
My team and I used pure javascript and some html to create the game. We held weekly meetings to delegate tasks and discuss priorities and or design decisions. Since we had no artists or sound designers, the game was created with programmer art and no sound.
